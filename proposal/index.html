Welcome to Blera

Rasna invites you into the real rhythm of life in the Tuscia hills — the work, the food, and the traditions that local families still carry on today. This is not a sightseeing tour. It's a few days spent alongside the people of Blera, doing what they do, eating what they grow, and learning crafts passed down for generations.

Below are the experiences available for your visit. Select the ones that interest you most — we'll build your itinerary around them.

Choose Your Experiences

Join a local family in the groves for a hands-on morning of olive picking, followed by a visit to the press to see the oil made — and tasted — fresh.

A late-summer tradition: pick ripe tomatoes from the field and spend the day making passata the old way, with the family that grew them.

Work alongside Renzo, a local artisan, to hand-craft and take home your own leather bag — a skill and souvenir in one.

See how traditional cured meats are made and aged, with a tasting of salumi straight from the curing room.

Explore the countryside around Civitella Cesi on horseback, through the woods and pastures that surround Blera.

A guided walk through the rock-cut Etruscan tombs and the ancient Via Clodia, tracing the roots of this land's first people — the Rasna.
