# Rasna — Pilot Experience
## Lean Cost Model | Group of 6 | 5 Days / 4 Nights

*This is the zero-fluff version. The goal of the pilot is to test the experience, lock in partner relationships, and get content (photos, testimonials) — not to make money. Run it at cost or just above. Friends and trusted contacts only.*

---

## Pilot Objectives

1. Confirm which activities actually work with a real group
2. Build trust with local partners (Renzo, Emiliano, grove owner, Pino)
3. Identify what to cut, improve, or reorder
4. Get photos, video, and honest written feedback for the website
5. Test the van logistics (pickup, daily driving, timing)

### Day 2 — Olive Harvest & Panonto

| Time | Activity | Cost |
|------|----------|------|
| 07:30 | Self-catered breakfast at B&B | €35 |
| 08:00 | Drive to grove | — |
| 08:15 | **Harvest** — rake, sort, net. Midway break: bread, oil, water | €150 (grove) |
| 12:30 | Drive to frantoio | — |
| 12:45 | **Oil pressing** — watch, taste, bottle each | €130 (pressing + bottles) |
| 13:30 | **Panonto** — back at the grove. Light the brace. Salsicce, pane, olio, vino | €80 |
| 15:30 | Return to B&B | — |
| 17:00 | Walk to necropolis (optional) | Free |
| 19:30 | Dinner | Beccone — €180 |

> **Contingency — it rains on Day 2:**
> The harvest can happen in light rain — it's messy but authentic, and guests will remember it. Heavy rain: postpone harvest to Day 3 morning (swap with Renzo, who moves to Day 2 afternoon indoors). Panonto under rain doesn't work — move to a covered lunch at Beccone (€60 extra, simple menu). Flag this scenario to the grove owner in advance: "what do we do if it rains on our day?" Get their answer before the trip.

> **Contingency — olives not ready (harvest window off by a week or two):**
> This is the highest-probability risk of the whole trip. Confirm with the grove owner 2 weeks before AND 3 days before. If olives aren't ready: replace harvest with a full San Giovenale hike (Pino leads, half day, free), visit the frantoio anyway to see the machinery and taste last year's oil, and do the panonto as a standalone event in the evening with Pino's brace. It's not the same, but it's still a coherent day.

> **Contingency — someone doesn't want to do physical harvest work:**
> Don't push. They can help with sorting (seated), watch from the edge, or take photos. Assign them the "official photographer" role. Most people who say they're not up for it end up joining once they see the others — but don't make it awkward if they don't.

> **Note on Renzo's timing:** If the leather workshop is scheduled for Day 3 morning, brief Renzo on scope before the day. A card wallet takes 2h with 6 people; a belt takes 4h+. For the pilot, agree on a simple object — keyring or card wallet — so the session ends on time and the afternoon isn't lost.

## Group Dynamics — What To Watch For

*Small groups amplify everything — good and bad. This is a 5-day trip in a van with 6 people.*

| Situation | What to do |
|-----------|-----------|
| One person dominates conversations with locals | Gently rotate who asks questions. Brief the group before Renzo and Emiliano: "let him explain first, then ask." |
| Two people in the group have tension | Don't take sides. Give everyone independent free time every afternoon — this is built into the schedule. |
| Someone is clearly not enjoying an activity | Acknowledge it privately, don't make it a group moment. Give them something useful to do — photos, carrying things, talking to the host. |
| The group is having a great moment | Stop. Don't narrate it or move on too quickly. Let it breathe. These are the moments that become testimonials. |
| Someone gets hurt (blisters, twisted ankle, minor cut at Renzo's) | First aid kit in the van. Know where the nearest pharmacy (farmacia) is in Blera. Hospital: Ospedale di Viterbo, ~25 min. |

*This is as important as the experience itself.*

- [ ] Photo of every activity (harvest, panonto fire, press, leather, affettati board, lake)
- [ ] Short video: oil coming out of the press, the brace being lit, Renzo's hands on the leather
- [ ] Note exact timings — when each session actually started and ended vs planned
- [ ] Note any moment where energy dropped or someone disengaged
- [ ] Note any moment where the group went quiet in a good way
- [ ] Written feedback from each guest within 48h of returning (5 questions max: what was the highlight? what would you cut? would you recommend it? what would you pay? one word for the trip)
- [ ] Confirm with each partner: do they want to do it again, and at what rate?
