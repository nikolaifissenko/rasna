About Rasna

An authentic Italy experience, built for small groups

Most "Italy experiences" you'll find online are the same handful of packaged day tours through Rome, Florence, and the Amalfi Coast — big buses, fixed scripts, a photo stop instead of a real one. Rasna is different: it's a small-group trip into Blera, a 2,800-year-old Etruscan hill town in Tuscia, northern Lazio, guided by the people who actually live there. You harvest the olives, not watch someone else harvest them. You eat at a family's table, not a tourist restaurant. Groups are capped at 6–10 guests, by design, so the experience stays real instead of turning into a show.

If you're looking for an authentic Italy travel experience beyond the postcard circuit — real people, real food, real land, in one of Italy's oldest and least-visited corners — this is built for exactly that.

Questions

Before you book.

What is Rasna?

Rasna is a small-group, all-inclusive travel experience into Blera, an ancient Etruscan hill town in Italy's Tuscia region (northern Lazio). Guests harvest olives, explore rock-cut Etruscan tombs, and share meals with local families — a hands-on alternative to typical Italy sightseeing tours.

How is this different from a normal Italy tour?

Most Italy tours move large groups through sights by bus. Rasna caps every group at 6–10 people and builds the trip around real local life — you work alongside the people who actually farm the land, cure the meat, and cook the meals, rather than watching a staged demonstration.

What's included in the price?

€1,450 per person, all-inclusive: accommodation, all meals, every activity you select, guided Etruscan site visits, and local transport from Rome or Viterbo. The only extra costs are flights, travel insurance, and personal spending.

Is this trip good for solo travelers?

Yes. Rasna is built for small groups of travelers who book individually, not just couples or private groups. Group sizes are capped at 6–10 total guests.

When does the next departure leave?

The next fixed departure is November 9–15, 2026, with limited spots. You can also book your own custom dates if that week doesn't work for you.

Where is Blera / Tuscia?

Blera is in Tuscia, the historic Etruscan region of northern Lazio, about 90 minutes from Rome — far enough to avoid the Tuscany and Amalfi Coast tourist crowds, close enough for an easy pickup from Rome or Viterbo.
