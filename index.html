Before Rome, there was Rasna.

"Rasna" is what the Etruscans called themselves — the civilization that carved cities into volcanic rock here nearly three millennia ago. Their tombs still line the ravines. Their roads still cut through the forest. We take a handful of guests at a time to live among what they left behind.

Why we stay small

This is not mass tourism.

We take 10 people at a time — because that's how many can actually fit around a family's table, or work a real olive grove without turning it into a show.

No staged performances. No gift shops. No Instagram setups. You'll be sweating in an olive grove, getting flour on your hands, eating dinner with the people whose land you worked. The scale stays small so the experience stays real — and rare.
