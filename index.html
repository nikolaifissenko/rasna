Blera, Northern Lazio

Rasna

Live the ancient rhythm of an Italian village — not as a tourist, but as a guest.

"Rasna" is what the Etruscans called themselves — the people who first carved this land from tufo, nearly 2,800 years ago. Their tombs, their roads, their spirit still live here. So does ours.

The Experiences

A few things,
done truly.

  • Autumn
    The Olive Harvest
    Pick olives by hand with a local family, press them at the frantoio, taste the new oil on bread grilled over fire.
  • Summer
    The Sauce Day
    August tomatoes, a wood fire, and the ritual of making passata — the way families here have done it for generations.
  • Year-round
    Leather & Craft
    Work leather under the hands of a local artisan. Cut, stitch, and leave with a bag you made yourself.
  • Year-round
    The Etruscan Walk
    Rock-cut tombs, the ancient Via Clodia, and the silence of a landscape that has barely changed in millennia.
  • Year-round
    Cheese & Cured Meats
    Visit a small producer in Civitella Cesi. Watch, taste, learn — then sit down to eat what you just saw made.
Our Promise

Intentionally small.

We are not building the next Positano. We stay small on purpose — so the experience stays real.

No buses. No crowds. No staged performances. Just a few guests at a time, welcomed into the actual daily life of a village that has been here since the Etruscans carved it from stone.
