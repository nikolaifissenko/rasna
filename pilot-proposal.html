Blera, Lived — Not Just Visited

A pilot proposal for a small-group, fully-hosted experience in the heart of Lazio's Tuscia — created with you, by Nikolai Fissenko and Maria Grazia.

Three Ways to Have Fun

This trip isn't just four days in Blera. It's an experience designed around three stages — and you're part of all three, with Nikolai as your host and guide throughout.

1

Planning It

Before you arrive: a personal welcome from Nikolai, a call to shape the itinerary together, and a "what to expect" guide so the anticipation is part of the fun.

2

Doing It

Four days of real, hands-on local life — harvests, workshops, meals with local families, and the landscape around Blera — guided every step of the way.

3

Remembering It

After the trip: a curated photo/video recap, the recipes from the workshops you did, and a standing invitation into the Blera alumni circle.

The Activities

  • Olive harvest — pick olives alongside the people who do it every year, then taste the oil they press.
  • Tomato & "sauce day" — harvest, cook, and bottle passata the way local families still do it.
  • Cured meats with Emiliano — see how traditional affettati are made and cured, with a tasting.
  • Leather bag-making with Renzo — make your own leather bag, hands-on, under his guidance.
  • Hand-made pasta workshop new — roll and shape your own pasta from scratch, then eat what you made.
  • Pizza-making workshop new — stretch, top, and bake your own pizza, wood-fired where possible.
  • Traditional dessert-baking new — bake classic local sweets with a local host and take some home.
  • Etruscan tombs & Via Clodia — guided walks through the rock-cut tombs and ancient roads around Blera.
  • Day trips — Lago di Vico, Villa Lante, Tarquinia, or Viterbo, as a local would show a friend around.

Sample 4-Day Pilot Itinerary

Day 1 — Arrival & Welcome

Transfer to Blera, welcome dinner with a local family, orientation walk through town with Nikolai.

Day 2 — Land & Table

Olive harvest (or seasonal equivalent) in the morning, hand-made pasta workshop in the afternoon, dinner with what you cooked.

Day 3 — Artisans & Exploration

Morning with Renzo (leatherwork) or Emiliano (affettati), afternoon excursion to Etruscan tombs / Via Clodia, evening pizza-making.

Day 4 — Slow Morning & Departure

Traditional dessert-baking session, farewell brunch, recap conversation about the trip and what comes next, transfer out.
